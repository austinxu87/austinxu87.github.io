About Me

I'm a first year ECE PhD student at Georgia Tech, where I am concentrating in machine learning. I am fortunate to be advised by Mark Davenport. I graduated with a BSE in Electrical Engineering from the University of Michigan in May 2019 (Go Blue!).

My work involves learning from pairwise comparisons and ranking systems. Specifically, I'm interested in the effect of utilizing human-in-loop pairwise/ranking feedback in generative models and reinforcement learning. Broadly, I am interested in studying (human) decision making from an interpretable and mathematically grounded perspective.

In my free time, I enjoy cooking (and eating), reading, running, and watching basketball (NBA and college). I'm also an urban transit enthusiast and interested in politics.
